Whiteboards are underrated!
  • 8 Jun, 2019
  • 2 minutes read

From time to time your collegues will ask you questions, you will have discussions or you just want to point out this insanely good idea which will finally solve all the problems the team was facing for such a long time. You are really trying your best to get your opposites to follow you along while explaining, but somehow, however and how often you try to explain it, they will not understand it.

Everyone know this situation: you try your best to explain, but noone understands you.

From my point of view there is an easy fix for this: Whiteboards.

1. Visually explain

Depending on what you want to explain you can go with different approaches here:

  • Explaining what this part of the code does:
    Draw a flow chart
  • Software states:
    Draw a table with the different states, when they will be changed and what will happen for each one of them
  • UI/UX:
    Draw mockups

2. Communicate and interact

Whiteboards are interactive tools! Someone can start explaining his idea and other people can just join.

Give everyone a pen and just start drawing!

3. Brainstorm

Just walk up and add your thoughts to a mindmap, a list or anything else. Improve upon on it. Change it. There are almost no boundries on what you can do.

So why are they underated?

They are underated in the sense that a lot of people just got one in theire office and use it from day to day, but seldomly appriciate how good of a tool it is.

A few days ago I realized that and that is why I wanted to share it with the world 😜
